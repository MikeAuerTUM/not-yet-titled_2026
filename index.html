On Academic Activism and STARs

Background and Context to the Interactive Installation

(scroll down to leave feedback or any other message)

In the seminar 'Applying Critical Theories in the Social Sciences', we read the paper 'The university as an infinite game' (Harré et al., 2017).
Like others in Critical University Studies, this paper discusses aspects of the current neoliberal university and criticizes the amount of competitiveness, that can get in the way of the shared goals and values that academia has. In it, the authors call for more academic activism, give their own perspectives from their academic lives, and also propose STARs as one possible part of academic activism.

STARs, an acronym for 'slow, tiny, acts of resistance' are described as the smaller actions that someone can take or things they can do in everyday life (such as putting up sticky notes to shine a light on something, sharing resources with others, or asking critical questions even if that could make it uncomfortable), alongside bigger "acts of resistance". Ideally, they can be creative and fun.

I chose to do my creative final project for the seminar inspired by this, because, while there are many things that annoy me and that I sometimes even would like to do something about them, I often feel like any action I would be doing could be bigger and more thought through, resulting in me actually not doing anything most of the time.
(I think I still often talk to friends about it, which also is doing something! But still, I would like to reach others as well.)

In doing my project as an interactive installation, it could itself then be seen as a STAR, hopefully having been / being fun for you, and possibly even inspiring some to question more, including what can/could be possible at (our) university, what we would like it to be (or maybe also what currently annoys us), and to also take more smaller actions where we would like to. Because together, they can add to a larger picture; and transform current structures towards something we want them to be.


And of course, never limited to university :)



Note
This is meant as an explanation and background to my interactive installation "Not yet titled (Stars)". Large parts are written from memory, only for this purpose. For any more scientific/academic reference and true reflection of the paper, please reference/read the paper itself :)

Additionally, while I like the metaphor of STARs, I do not agree with all parts of the paper, and have criticism on it too.
